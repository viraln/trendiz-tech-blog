## Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA): Your First Line of Defense

MFA is no longer a luxury; it's a necessity.  While passwords remain a critical component, relying solely on them is like locking your front door with a flimsy latch.  MFA adds an extra layer of security by requiring a second form of verification, such as a one-time code sent to your phone or a biometric scan.

> **EXPERT INSIGHT:**  Don't just enable MFA; understand the different types (OTP, biometrics, security keys) and choose the options that best suit your needs and devices.

* ‚úÖ **Implement MFA on all critical accounts:** Email, banking, social media, and online shopping accounts should all have MFA enabled.
* üîë **Use different MFA methods:** Don't rely on just one type of MFA. Combining methods significantly enhances security.
* üõ°Ô∏è **Consider security keys:**  Hardware security keys offer the strongest form of MFA protection, providing an extra layer of protection against phishing and other attacks.

* ‚ö° **Change default passwords:**  Never use the default passwords provided by manufacturers.  Create strong, unique passwords for each device.
